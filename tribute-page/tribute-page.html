At a Glance

The Legacy

Robin Williams was an American stand-up comedian and actor that started as a stand-up comedian in the mid 1970s. After rising to fame as Mork in Mork & Mindy , Williams went on to establish a career in both stand-up comedy and feature film acting.

After his first starring film role in Popeye (1980), he went on to build an extensive career in front of a camera. Williams won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor, two Emmy Awards, seven Golden Globe Awards, two Screen Actors Guild Awards, and five Grammy Awards throughout his career.

Robin was considered a "national treasure" by many in the entertainment industry and by the public.His on-stage energy and improvisational skill became a model for a new generation of stand-up comedians and his film performances often influenced other actors, both in and out of the film industry. According to media scholar Derek A. Burrill, "probably the most important contribution he made to pop culture, across so many different media, was as Robin Williams the person."

The Man

Robin McLaurin Williams was born in Chicago, Illinois on July 21, 1951 to his father Robert Fitzgerald Williams a senior executive at Ford Motor Company and his mother Laurie McLaurin a former model from Jackson, Mississippi. He attended public elementary and middle school in Lake Forest, Illinois. Later, when Williams was 16, his family moved to California where Robin attended Redwood High School and was first introduced to Drama class.

Williams then studied theatre for three years at the College of Marin community college after which he would gain a full scholarship to Juilliard School in New York City. Here was accepted into the Advanced Program at the school that year.

During his time in Community College Robin would meet his first wife Valerie Velardi bring his first child into the world, Zachary "Zak" Williams. He would later have two more children Zelda Williams and Cody Williams by his second wife Marsha Garces. Williams stated, "My children give me a great sense of wonder. Just to see them develop into these extraordinary human beings."

Notable Body of Work

Robin Williams Live at the Roxy (1978)

Robin Williams Live at the Roxy was Robin's first HBO special, part of the HBO On Location series. It was taped on location at the Roxy Theatre in Hollywood in 1978. This stand up special showcases the early talent and comedic prowess that would catapult him into a long career in the entertainment industry.

Mork&Mindy (1978-1982)

The Mork&Mindy television series aired on ABC from September 14, 1978 to May 27, 1982 and is Robin Williams' first major acting role, which became famous for Williams' use of his manic improvisational comedic talent.

Robin Williams A Night at the Met (1986)

In Robin Williams A Night at the Met he's the Pavarotti of Puns, the Domingo of Drollery, the Caruso of Comedy. It's comedy at the speed of light, with more LPMs than any comedy special ever. Robin Williams, at his most outrageous in a one-man assault on the funny bone. Strap yourself in for a flight on the "Concorde of Comedy" as you take off for the reaches of the absurd. Take a fantastic ride down with Williams' stream-of-consciousnes.

Good Morning Vietnam (1987)

In Good Morning Vietnam radio funny man Adrian Cronauer (Robin Williams) is sent to Vietnam to bring a little comedy back into the lives of the soldiers. After setting up shop, Cronauer delights the G.I.s but shocks his superior officer with his irreverent take on the war. While Cronauer's superior officer attempts to censor Cronauer's broadcasts, Cronauer pursues a relationship with a Vietnamese girl, who shows him the horrors of war first-hand.

Mrs.Doubtfire (1993)

In Mrs. Doubtfire, a comedy-drama film, Robin Williams plays the role of a recently divorced actor who dresses up as a female housekeeper to be able to interact with his children. The film recieved mixed reviews after its release in 1993 but that didn't stop Robin from being awarded the Golden Globe for Best Actor.

Patch Adams (1998)

The Patch Adams comedy-drama film starring Robin Williams based on the life story of Dr. Hunter "Patch" Adams. Robin proves once again that his improvisational approach to comedy can heal the wounds of the soul and bring a film character to full depth with his dramatic acting styles.

Weapons of Self Destruction (2009)

The Weapons of Self Destruction comedy special was his first solo special in seven years, Williams covers such topics as global warming, sex and politics, the state of health care in the country (suggesting a cash for clunkers program for elderly relatives, among other things), drugs - recreational and otherwise - and more personal topics, including his recent heart surgery.
